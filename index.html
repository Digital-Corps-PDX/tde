Trust & Decision Engineering

A research and teaching practice rooted at the University of Michigan
Supported by State Capacity AI

Trust & Decision Engineering studies how public decisions move through institutional systems and how those systems shape outcomes.

Public decisions travel through policy, software, interpretation, and administrative process. These layers form the civil stack. The lab maps these layers and studies how authority moves through them.

Current work examines decision routing, institutional interpretation, administrative incentives, and automated authority in public systems.

Frameworks

The Civil Stack The layers through which public decisions travel.
Judgment Routing How responsibility and discretion move through institutions.
Trust Forensics Tracing how systems shape institutional legitimacy.
Automated Authority Designing contestable and legible algorithmic decisions.
